Theme

This game is eventually going to be based on a class I took last semester called Operating Systems. It was a gruelling class that's left me with a bitter feeling -- I learned so much, yet I feel it was taught horribly.

Not only that, it's seen as a terrifying class by everyone. I want to see if making a game about it, turning the experience into fuel for something else I enjoy, can help me resolve my feelings (plus, make the class seem less intimidating for others).

Because of this theme, some notation I'll use, like powerups being called things like 'Vanish' and 'Exec', are OS-specific -- I don't expect you to understand what they mean just from playing the prototype.

How to Play



You're a white ball on a graph. You can move with the left and right arrow keys to slide along whatever edge you're perched on. At junctures and dead ends, you'll need to choose which direction to travel in by pressing the key that pops up on a circular HUD.

Some junctures have special events sitting on them, called things like 'Vanish' and 'Exec'. When these words pop up on your HUD, you can activate them with the space bar.

Vanish destroys the graph you're on (in game, this will be the equivalent of beating the level). Exec teleports the player back to the graph's beginning. When these words pop up on your HUD, you can activate them with the space bar.

You have multiple graphs to complete. You can swap between them at any time by pressing 's'.

The objective is to try to navigate the entire graph. There's no win/lose condition right now, as I've spent so much time just trying to get the framework of the game ready (don't get me started on the UI), but it's enough to see the big gameplay mechanics that'll be in there.

Needless to say it still needs a lot of work, but it's been an enriching experience.
